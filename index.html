Who This Is For

If your operation is stuck, the bottleneck is one of four things.

01

The Sub-$1M Owner-Operator

Currently riding a mower or running a route three days a week. Crew of 1 to 5. Revenue $150K to $700K. The cohort walks pricing, estimating, and the owner-operator transition out of the truck in the first four weeks.

02

The $500K-$2M Systems-Installer

Crew of 5 to 15. Ops getting away from you. Maybe Home.works, maybe Jobber, maybe ServiceTitan, maybe a spreadsheet. The cohort installs the operating system that lets you step out of the day-to-day.

03

The Career-Changer Crossing Into The Trades

Coming from corporate, sales, or another industry. Wants to start home-service in the next 6 to 12 months. The cohort walks the independent-versus-franchise decision before you commit to either route.

The Work

Four modules. Eight weeks.

No video-on-demand library. The whole thing is built around weekly live group calls and scheduled office hours.

Module One · Weeks 1-2

Sales, Pricing, And Estimating

By the end of week 2, you have a pricing structure, an estimating process that does not depend on you being on site, and the bid template that closes the right percentage of jobs at the right margin.

The pricing structure I run across residential and commercial work. The estimating process that does not depend on you driving to every property. The bid template that holds against undercut competitors. Your specific operation goes into the live call. The pricing gets pressure-tested against your route density.

Module Two · Weeks 3-4

Crew, Recruiting, And Retention

By the end of week 4, your sourcing channels, vetting bar, and 90-day retention SOP are written, and your existing crew has a hold-or-coach call on each name.

Why crews ghost at month three, and the SOP that holds them. The retention conversation script. The post-launch crew review at week 6. How to read first-30-days data and predict month-6 churn. Your specific crew goes on the underwriting table. The hold-or-coach call gets documented.

Module Three · Weeks 5-6

Ops, KPIs, And Software Install

By the end of week 6, your KPI cadence, weekly operations rhythm, and software stack are locked, with the next 1-2 hires scoped.

The three numbers on the dashboard before the laptop opens. The weekly KPI cadence. The software install, your existing CRM or Home.works or whatever you are running, set up to feed the cadence and not the other way around. Your operations doc gets the audit on the live call. The next 90-day plan gets written.

Module Four · Weeks 7-8

Owner-Operator Transition

By the end of week 8, you have a 90-day plan for stepping off the truck, replacing yourself on estimating, and the next role you are stepping into.

How to step off the truck without the wheels coming off. Replacing yourself on estimating, then on operations, then on sales. The 90-day plan for the role you are stepping into next. Your content plan gets the live audit. The next 30 days of operator-mode work get scheduled.

Live group call once a week with Mike. Two scheduled office-hours blocks per month for 1:1 questions. Private community channel for the 8 weeks. Templates, scripts, SOPs, recruiting docs, and P and L worksheets handed off as you complete each module.

FAQ

Common questions, before applying.

How is this different from the free courses on mikeandes.com?

The free courses are the framework. The cohort is the install. The courses teach you what to look for; the cohort walks your specific operation on a live group call. Many cohort members finish the free Landscape Business Course or the free MBA-for-Entrepreneurs course first and come in already knowing the vocabulary.

Is this the Augusta franchise?

No. The cohort is operator mentorship, open to operators in any home-service trade and to career-changers from any industry. The Augusta franchise is a separate path at augustalawncareservices.com for operators who want a full business-model change. Cohort grads who choose that route are routed there; cohort grads who want continued mentorship route to 1-1 work instead. The cohort feeds the franchise; it does not replace it.

How fast will I see results?

Most cohort members name their primary bottleneck in week 1 and have the first SOP rewritten by week 3. The crew or retention rewrite lands by week 6. The full owner-operator transition map lands by week 8. Results vary by market, season, and operator execution.

Is this a course?

No. It is mentorship. Weekly live group calls with Mike, scheduled office-hours blocks for 1:1 questions, private community channel for the 8 weeks. There is no video-on-demand library to fall behind on.

What is the time commitment?

3-4 hours per week for 8 weeks. One live group call per week (60-90 min). Office hours optional. The work between calls is your real operation, not homework.

What is the investment?

Two tiers in the mid-tier mentorship range. Standard tier covers the 8-week cohort, weekly group calls, office hours, and templates. Standard-plus-operating-review tier adds a 1:1 review of your existing operation and three follow-up 1:1 calls in the 90 days post-cohort. Hint range, not an invoice. The application surfaces exact pricing for the cycle you apply to.

Do I have to use Home.works?

No. Module 3 walks the software install, and Home.works is one of three CRM options on the table. The cohort runs cleanly on whatever CRM you are already on. Operators on Jobber, ServiceTitan, HouseCallPro, or anything else walk Module 3 with their existing stack.

What if I am still under $300K revenue?

The sub-$1M owner-operator is one of the three primary avatars. Week 1 starts at pricing, estimating, and the route-density math. Week 8 ends at a 90-day plan for the next operator stage. If you are pre-revenue, the free Landscape Business Course is the right first step; the cohort routes you back after you have a real operating month or two in.

What if I already run a $2M+ business?

The cohort handles the scaling case at the top of its range. Modules 3 and 4 walk the systems and the owner-operator transition for a $1M+ operation. The standard-plus-operating-review tier adds a 1:1 audit. If you are already past $5M, the conversation is the Augusta franchise or 1-1 mentorship, not the cohort.

How does this work with the Augusta franchise or 1-1 coaching?

The cohort is the productised mid-tier. If you want continued mentorship after the cohort, that route is 1-1 mentorship. If you want a full business-model change into the Augusta system, that route is the franchise application at augustalawncareservices.com. The cohort feeds both; it does not replace either.

Refund policy?

Yes. Complete the first two weeks, decide it is not for you, full refund within 14 days of cohort kickoff. No questions.

Why do I have to apply?

You do not have to. The application form is four minutes and it just helps size cohorts and route the office-hours topics to what is actually on members' desks this cycle. Submit the form, you get straight to enrolment, no setter calls, no back-and-forth.
